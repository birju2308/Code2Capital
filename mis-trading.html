What is MIS in Trading?

Complete Guide for Beginners

MIS stands for Margin Intraday Square-off. It is an order type used in intraday trading where traders buy or sell stocks and close the position on the same day before the market closes.

Many brokers such as Zerodha, Upstox, and Angel One provide MIS trading options to allow traders to use margin and trade with a larger amount of money.

MIS is only for intraday trading and positions cannot be carried to the next day.

Example of MIS Trading

Suppose you have ₹10,000 in your trading account. If the broker provides 5x margin, you can trade stocks worth ₹50,000 using MIS.

  • Your capital: ₹10,000
  • Broker margin: 5x
  • Total trading value: ₹50,000

Key Features of MIS Orders

  • Used only for intraday trading
  • Provides leverage or margin
  • Positions must be closed the same day
  • Broker auto square-off before market closing

Advantages of MIS Trading

  • Trade with smaller capital
  • Higher profit potential
  • More trading opportunities

Disadvantages of MIS Trading

  • Higher risk because of leverage
  • Positions cannot be carried overnight
  • Losses can increase quickly

Conclusion

MIS (Margin Intraday Square-off) is an order type used for intraday trading with leverage. It allows traders to take larger positions with less capital, but it also involves higher risk. Beginners should always use stop-loss and proper risk management while trading.
